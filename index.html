A prestigious National Dialogue and Book Launch Ceremony is being organised under the joint auspices of The Film Foundation Trust (New Delhi), Anang Prakashan (Delhi), Syahi Blue Books (Delhi), Sahitya Unnayan Sansthan (Chandausi), and Bundelkhand Sahitya Unnayan Samiti (Jhansi). Our Purpose

Vision & Mission

We endeavour to provide meaningful, wholesome, healthy cinema to the Indian audience — prioritising the promotion of Indian culture, ethics, and national heritage as a powerful alternative to the overemphasis on foreign culture in current films, OTT, and television content.

TFFT operates strictly on a no profit – no loss basis. Every rupee raised flows directly into cultural, educational, and social programmes across India.

What We Do

Our Main Objects

01🎬

Film Production & Distribution

Produce, exhibit, and distribute cinematographic & television films. Operate studios, OTT platforms, and theatres showcasing culturally enriching content.

02🎓

Education & Skill Development

Run schools, colleges, vocational training centres, and online cinema education programmes. Provide scholarships and propagate cinematic expertise nationally.

03🎭

Cultural Events & Festivals

Organise film festivals, art exhibitions, dance events, thematic shows, and award programmes celebrating India's heritage and cultural values.

04📚

Publications & Media

Publish books, magazines, journals, and newspapers — online and offline — to spread cinematic, cultural, and educational knowledge across India.

05🤝

Social Welfare & Relief

Provide relief during natural calamities; support rural, tribal, and urban poor communities. Champion human rights and first-generation entrepreneurship.

06🔬

Research & Advocacy

Undertake socio-scientific research, collaborate with national and international agencies, and advocate for cultural, ethical, and scientific prosperity.

TFFT Plans

Lens on Orchha

A 3-Day Immersive Short Film Workshop  ·  Orchha, Jhansi

⚠️  The dates below are proposed and subject to confirmation. Final schedule will be announced.

Set against the stunning backdrop of Orchha's forts, Betwa River, and cenotaphs — this immersive workshop is planned to transform aspiring filmmakers into storytellers. By the final day every group is expected to walk away with a finished short film in their portfolio, shot in a living heritage landscape.

Day 1  —  Proposed

Foundation & Language of Cinema

Screenwriting, visual grammar, shot sizes, camera & sound toolkit, location scout & pre-visualisation at Orchha Fort complex.

Day 2  —  Proposed

The Shoot — Lights, Camera, Action!

Golden-hour shoots at Riverbank, Fort & Village lanes. Blocking, staging, 180° rule, 3-point lighting, interview setups & B-roll capture.

Day 3  —  3 May 2026 (Proposed)

Post-Production & The Premiere

DaVinci Resolve editing, colour grading, sound design, festival strategy — culminating in a live premiere screening & certificate distribution.
